# SEL Story Templates Catalog

Complete reference of all 25 built-in SEL story templates organized by CASEL competency.

## Overview of CASEL Competencies

The 5 core social-emotional learning competencies developed by CASEL (Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning):

1. **Self-Awareness** - Recognizing and understanding one's own emotions, thoughts, strengths, and values
2. **Self-Management** - Managing emotions, thoughts, and behaviors effectively to achieve goals
3. **Social Awareness** - Understanding and empathizing with others' perspectives, feelings, and needs
4. **Relationship Skills** - Building and maintaining positive relationships through communication
5. **Responsible Decision-Making** - Making ethical and constructive choices considering consequences

## COMPETENCY 1: SELF-AWARENESS 🪞

Recognizing emotions, accurate self-perception, understanding strengths/limitations.

### Template 1: The Feeling Detective

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Icon** | 🔍 |
| **Age Range** | 3-7 years |
| **Difficulty** | Gentle |
| **Story Goals** | Emotion recognition, self-observation |
| **Theme** | A curious character learns to identify emotions by observing facial expressions and physical sensations |
| **Key Skills** | Emotion labeling, noticing feelings in self and others |
| **Character Type** | Detective with magnifying glass |
| **Setting** | Everyday situations (home, playground) |
| **Duration** | 3-5 paragraphs |
| **Emotional Focus** | Happy, sad, worried, calm, excited |

**Story Premise:** A young detective discovers that everyone has feelings written on their faces and bodies. By observing how people look and move, the detective can understand their emotions.

**Learning Outcome:** Children learn to recognize emotions in facial expressions and body language, developing emotional vocabulary.

## Usage in Sessions

Each template can be used:
1. **Individual therapy:** Child reads story, therapist discusses feelings
2. **Parent guidance:** Parent reads with child at home
3. **Classroom learning:** Teacher uses with groups
4. **Progress tracking:** Monitor which competencies child engages with
5. **Emotional check-in:** Use story as springboard for discussion

All templates are designed to open dialogue naturally and age-appropriately.
